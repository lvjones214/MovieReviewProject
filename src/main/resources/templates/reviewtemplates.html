Rush Hour

Genre: Action

Year Released: 1998

MPAA Rating: PG-13

Star Rating: 5 stars

Hashtags: Assign Hashtags

Description: When a Chinese diplomat's daughter is kidnapped in Los Angeles, he calls in Hong Kong Detective Inspector Lee (Jackie Chan) to assist the FBI with the case. But the FBI doesn't want anything to do with Lee, and they dump him off on the LAPD, who assign wisecracking Detective James Carter (Chris Tucker) to watch over him. Although Lee and Carter can't stand each other, they choose to work together to solve the case on their own when they figure out they've been ditched by both the FBI and police

Review: Jackie Chan and Chris Tucker were great in these movies. I loved all 3 of them. The two of them were hilarious throughout, and the fighting scenes were pretty decent, too. I especially love the outtakes during the end credits, where Mr. Chan has trouble speaking a few words in English, while Mr. Tucker has REAL trouble speaking Chinese. I think he gets better at it, though. I love that in all 3 movies, the bad guy dies by falling to his death. The women were pretty good-looking, too. Would definitely recommend buying the whole trilogy on blu-ray online. The movies are just that good.
